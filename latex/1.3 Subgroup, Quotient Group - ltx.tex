\begin{lemma}
Let $G$ be a topological group, and $H$ be a subgroup.

(a) If $G$ is cpt then $H$ and $G/H$ are both cpt.

(b) If $G$ is locally cpt then $H$ and $G/H$ have both locally cpt.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}

(a) $H$ is a closed subset of a cpt set and $G/H = \pi(G)$ where $G$ is cpt and $\pi$ is continuous mapping. Hence a continuous map of cpt set is cpt.

(b) A closed subset of a locally cpt set is locally cpt.

Let $S$ be locally cpt, $T = \overline{S}$ and $T \subseteq S$. For $p \in T \cap S$, there exists a nbhd of $p$ in $S$ $U_p$ st. $U_p$ is cpt. Hence $H$ is locally cpt.

Goal: $G/H$ is locally cpt.

\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}
For any topological group $G$, let $U$ be a nbhd of $e$, there exists a nbhd of $e$ $V$ st. $V \subseteq U$.
\begin{proof}
By property 5) and 6) we know that for every nbhd of $e$ $U$ we have a symmetric nbhd of $e$ st. $V^2 \subseteq U$. Now $x \in \overline{V} = \bigcap_{w \in V} wV$ where $V^2$ is set of all nbhd of $e$, this implies $x \in \overline{V} \subseteq V^2 \subseteq U$.
\end{proof}
\end{proposition}
