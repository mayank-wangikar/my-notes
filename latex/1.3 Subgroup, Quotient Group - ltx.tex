(i) $\overline{H}$ is a subgroup of top. group $G$

\[

\end{proof}
\end{proposition}

\begin{lemma}
Let $G$ be a topological group, and $H$ be a subgroup.
(a) If $G$ is compact then $H$ and $G \backslash H$ are both compact.
(b) If $G$ is locally compact then $H$ and $G \backslash H$ have both locally compact.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}

(a) $H$ is a closed subset of a compact set and $G/H = \pi(G)$ where $G$ is compact and $\pi$ is continuous mapping. Hence a continuous map of compact set is compact.

(b) A closed subset of a locally compact set is locally compact.

Let $S$ be locally compact, $T = \overline{S}$ and $T \subseteq S$. For $p \in T \cap S$, there exists a neighborhood of $p$ in $S$, $\overline{U_p}$ st. $\overline{U_p}$ is compact. Observe for $p \in T$ there exists an open set $T \cap U_p$ in $T$ such that $\overline{T \cap U_p} = \overline{T} \cap \overline{U_p}$ is compact as $\overline{T \cap U_p}$ is closed subset of $\overline{U_p}$ which is compact.

Hence $H$ is locally compact.

Goal: $G/H$ is locally compact.

\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}
For any topological group \( G \), let \( U \) be a neighborhood of \( e \), there exists a neighborhood of \( e \) \( V \) such that \( V^2 \subseteq U \).

\begin{proof}
By property 5) and 6) we know that for every neighborhood of \( e \) \( U \) we have a symmetric neighborhood of \( e \) \( V \) such that \( V^2 \subseteq U \). Now \( x \in \overline{V} = \bigcap_{w \in V^e} w \) where \( V^e \) is set of all neighborhoods of \( e \), this implies \( x \in \overline{V} \subseteq V^2 \subseteq U \).
\end{proof}
\end{proposition}
